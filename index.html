Tim Berners-Lee

The WWW's father

Important events throughout his life:

  • 1955: Born in London
  • 1969-1973: Begins elementary school at Sheet Mont and then goes to Emanuel School. Both in London
  • 1973-1976: Studies at Queen's College, Oxford University
  • 1978: Works at G. D. Nash Limited, where he writes an operating system
  • 1980: He works at CERN (Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire) proposing a project based on hypertext to facilitate the way of sharing information between researchers
  • 1989: He unites internet and hypertext, emerging the World Wide Web (WWW) but at that time it was not successful
  • 1990: With the help of Robert Cailliau, he carries out a review of the WWW that was finally accepted by his manager
  • 1991: The first web server found at CERN goes online
  • 1992-1995: The number of web servers went from 26 to 200, reflecting the high speed of Internet diffusion
  • 1994: Tim Berners-Lee enters the Massachusetts Institute of Technology Computer Science and Artifial Intelligence Laboratory. He moves to the United States and launches the W3C, which he currently leads.
  • 1996: The University of Southampton recognizes his contribution to developing the WWW
  • 1997: Hi is officially declared a member of the Order of the British Empire
  • 1999: His book "Waving the net" is pusblished where Lee explains why web technology is free. He is considered at the same time the investor and protector of the web
  • 2001: He is declared a member of the Royal Society
  • 2002: Receives a Japanese award. He is considered by the British public to be among the 100 Britons of all time and wins the Technology Festival Award in Telluride, Colorado
  • 2004: Receives the honorary degree of Doctor Science from the University of Lancaster
  • 2005:He is named Best British of the year for his achievements and his demonstration of key British qualities (humility, determination, and akeen sense of humor and adaptability)
  • 2006: He is named an honorary member of the Royal Society of Arts
  • 2007: He is the winner of the Charles Stark Draper Award
  • 2021: Continues working hard...
